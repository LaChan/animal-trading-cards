Snow Leopard

Snow Leopards generally live at 3500 meters, but in summer, the snow leopard may climb higher to cooler altitudes of as high as 5000 meters above sea level.

  • Scientific Name: Panthera uncia
  • Average Length: 75 to 150 cm (30 to 59 in)
  • Average Lifespan: 17 years
  • Habitat: Alpine and subalpine zones at elevations from 3,000 to 4,500 m (9,800 to 14,800 ft)

The snow leopard is a large cat native to the mountain ranges of Central and South Asia.The snow leopard inhabits alpine and subalpine zones at elevations from 3,000 to 4,500 m (9,800 to 14,800 ft), ranging from eastern Afghanistan to Mongolia and western China.The snow leopard's fur is whitish to gray with black spots on head and neck, but larger rosettes on the back, flanks and bushy tail. The belly is whitish, and the fur is thick with hairs between 5 and 12 cm long.
